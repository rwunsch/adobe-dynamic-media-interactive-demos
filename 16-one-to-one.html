Dynamic Media · 1-to-1 personalization

One template, a million personalized banners

A designer builds the product banner once — the layout, the fonts, the layer stack, the lighting. After that, marketing (or a personalization engine) generates unlimited 1-to-1 variants purely through URL parameters: swap the product cutout, rewrite the headline, change the price, restyle the colours. No new files, no round-trip to Photoshop. This is a real template on Adobe's public Viewers demo tenant: 1-to-1-template-square.

Product

Each option is a transparent product cutout asset. The template drops whichever one the $product parameter names into the layered composition.

Why 1-to-1

The template encapsulates the design — the layer stack, the fonts, the positioning. Consumers only supply data: a product, three strings, a couple of colours. Feed it a product catalog or a customer segment and generate thousands of personalized creatives from one source of truth.

Template parameters start with $ — they feed the named layers the designer wired up. Note the colour convention: $background_color takes a 0x prefix, while the text colours are bare hex. $headline accepts \par for line breaks; the type styling lives in the template, not the URL.
