Dynamic Media · Presets & Smart Crop

Two kinds of preset, one master file

A preset is a named, centrally-governed recipe applied on the fly to a single master asset. Below: first, brand treatments — colour and contrast looks owned by the brand team; second, the comparison that matters — a naive aspect crop that slices the middle of the frame versus a Smart Crop that understands where the subject is.

Master asset

One uploaded master. Every treatment below is applied to whichever asset you pick — zero pre-baked derivatives.

What a treatment is

Each tile applies a real, named tenant preset — a bundle of colour, contrast and sharpening rules — to the selected asset as ?$name$&wid=600. The look lives centrally: re-tune the recipe once and every asset that references the name updates. Marketers just drop the short name; the brand team owns the rules.

Governed centrally Zero pre-baked files

Same pixels in, nine governed looks out — all from one file. The &wid=600 is just there to keep the demo snappy; a pure preset at native size can hit the reply-size cap.

Dynamic Media · Smart Crop

Naive crop vs Smart Crop

An aspect-ratio preset has to throw away pixels to hit the target shape. A naive centre crop slices the middle of the frame — and decapitates the subject. A Smart Crop is content-aware: it finds the subject and keeps it framed at any ratio. Same master, same output size, side by side.

This section is fixed to the bus portrait — the four named Smart Crops (:11square :169banner :54vert :61banner) are configured only on that asset, so it does not follow the picker above.

The difference is the whole argument: a plain aspect preset (fit=crop) is blind to content and cuts off heads; Smart Crop keeps the subject in frame across every channel — square avatar, wide banner, tall story, ultra-wide hero — from one upload.
