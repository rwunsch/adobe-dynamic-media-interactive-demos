Dynamic Media · Adaptive video

Upload once. Stream to every device.

You drop a single high-bitrate master file into Dynamic Media and it does the rest: it automatically transcodes that one upload into a full adaptive bitrate ladder (multiple resolutions and bitrates), then serves them through one viewer and one URL. The player measures the viewer's network and device on the fly and picks the best rendition — switching up or down as bandwidth changes — all edge-cached on the same Scene7 CDN that delivers your images and 3D.

The pipeline

1 · Upload One master, e.g. 1280×720, 30 MB
2 · Auto-encode DM transcodes an HLS/DASH ladder
3 · Adaptive deliver Viewer picks the bitrate per request

Auto-generated renditions

From the single upload, the tenant encodes a ladder like the one below. The player starts conservatively, then climbs as it confirms headroom — and drops back instantly if the connection degrades.

…-0x400-800k …-0x600-2000k …-0x800-3000k

Each chip is one rung — height × bitrate. You never request a specific one; the adaptive manifest hands the player the whole ladder.

Why it matters

No manual encoding, no per-device files to manage, no separate video host. The same viewer embed works on a 5G phone and a corporate desktop — buffering on slow links, full quality on fast ones. Configure the look (controls, autoplay, captions) once on a Video Viewer Preset in DM and embed it everywhere.

Video Viewer embed URL

The asset is the master video on the tenant; the viewer reads the adaptive manifest and streams the right rendition. serverUrl and contenturl are the same image- and binary-delivery endpoints used by every other Dynamic Media asset.

Poster frame — a still pulled from the video

Point the image-serving URL at a video asset and Dynamic Media renders a poster frame on the fly — the same on-demand transform engine that resizes and reformats stills. One asset feeds both the player and the thumbnail.
